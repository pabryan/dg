Piecewise Regular Curves

A piecewise regular curve \(\gamma : [a, b] \to S\) is a continuous curve such that there exists a partition \[ a = t_0 < t_1 < \cdots < t_{k-1} < t_k = b \] with \(\gamma\) is regular on \([t_i, t_{i+1}]\). The points \(\gamma(t_i)\) are called the vertices.

Piecewise Regular Curves

Regular means differentiable and \(\gamma' \ne 0\) with left and right continuous limits: \(\lim_{t\to^+ t_i} \gamma'(t)\) and \(\lim_{t\to^- t_i} \gamma'(t)\) are defined and non-zero \[ \gamma'_-(t_i) = \lim_{t\to t_i^-} \gamma'(t), \quad \gamma'_+(t_i) = \lim_{t\to t_i^+} \gamma'(t). \]

\[ T_{\pm} (t_i) = \lim_{t\to t_i^{\pm}} T(t) \]

Angle

Define the angle \(\theta_i\) between \(\gamma'_-(t_i)\) and \(\gamma'_+(t_1)\) as follows:

  1. \[ \abs{\theta} = \abs{\arccos g(T_i^-, T_i^+)} \in (0, \pi). \] where \(T = \gamma'/\abs{\gamma'}\) is the unit tangent.
  2. We take \(\theta \in (-\pi, \pi)\) by choosing the sign so that \(\theta > 0\) whenever \[ \{T_i^-, T_i^+\} \] is positively oriented and \(\theta < 0\) otherwise.
  3. The case of a cusp is when \(\theta = \pi\) in which case it's possibly to choose the sign so that \(\theta\) varies continuously.

Gauss-Bonnet Theorem (Local)

Let \(D \subseteq S\) be homeomorphic to a disc with boundary a piecewise regular, simple, closed curve, \(\gamma\). Then \[ \int_D K dA + \int_{\gamma} \kappa ds = 2\pi - \sum_{i=1}^k \theta_i. \]

\(\gamma\) is only piecewise regular \(\Rightarrow\) \(\kappa\) not defined at vertices \[ \int_{\gamma} \kappa ds = \sum_{i=1}^k \int_{t_{i-1}}^{t_i} \kappa ds. \]

Gauss-Bonnet: Regular Plane Case

\(K \equiv 0\) so Gauss-Bonnet becomes \[ \int_{\gamma} \kappa ds = 2\pi - \sum_{i=1}^k \theta_i. \]

For \(\gamma\) regular (no vertices) Turning Tangents gives \[ \int_{\gamma} \kappa ds = 2\pi. \]

Gauss-Bonnet: Plane Case

For piecewise regular, break up the integral at the vertices:

\begin{equation*} \begin{split} \int_{\gamma} \kappa ds &= \sum \int_{t_{i-1}}^{t_i} \kappa ds = \sum \int_{t_{i-1}}^{t_i} \partial_s \theta ds = \sum \theta^-(t_i) - \theta^+(t_{i-1}) \\ &= \theta(t_k)^- - \theta(t_0)^+ + \sum \theta^-(t_i) - \theta^+(t_i) \\ &= 2\pi - \sum \theta_i. \end{split} \end{equation*}
